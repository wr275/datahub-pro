## Executive summary

The product is ahead of the business. You have a surface area most Series-A SaaS companies haven't built yet: KPI dashboards, pivots, forecasting, NPV, budgets, SharePoint, AI, admin, multi-tenant auth, usage metering, billing. What you don't have is **revenue, named design partners, or pipeline**.

For the next 90 days, every working hour goes into one of five activities:

1. Hiring the first commercial person
2. Defining who you sell to (ICP)
3. Locking down pricing so conversations don't stall
4. Landing 3-5 design partners from your warm network
5. Building an outbound engine with the new hire

Everything else — including new features, SEO, content marketing, performance tuning, and landing page iterations — is postponed until a paying customer specifically asks for it.

**The 90-day target:**

| Metric | End of month 1 | End of month 2 | End of month 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Design partners signed | 2 | 4 | 5 |
| Demos / week | 3 (you only) | 15 (with hire) | 20 |
| Paying customers | 0 | 2 | 5 |
| MRR | £0 | £300 | £1,000 |
| Hire status | In process | Started | Ramped |

MRR in month 3 is small on purpose. The goal is not revenue — it's **proof**. Five logos paying anything >£0 tells you the ICP is real, the pricing works, and the product closes. That's what unlocks the next phase (fundraising, content investment, bigger outbound team). Optimising for MRR this early makes you chase anyone with a credit card and destroys the ICP discipline.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Product positioning — horizontal, beachhead vertical

DataHub Pro is a **horizontal analytics product** — any non-technical SME with tabular data and a reporting rhythm can use it. But until we have 20 paying customers, we sell to **one vertical** only. This is how every successful horizontal SaaS has been launched (Airtable → film production · Notion → engineering docs · Retool → ops dashboards). Pick one beachhead, win it, then expand.

The next 90 days = **UK insight & market research agencies**. Everything — the cold emails, the demo, the case studies, the landing page's "Who it's for" block — speaks agency-language until we hit 5 paying agency logos. After that, we expand deliberately.

### The one-sentence ICP

> An **Insight Director, Research Director, or Head of Analytics** at a **UK-based market research or insight agency with 20-150 employees**, who spends **30-50% of their team's billable time turning raw survey / interview / behavioural data into client-ready PowerPoint and Excel deliverables**, and is feeling the **AI pressure from clients asking "how are you using AI to speed this up?"**

That's narrow enough to target, write to, and reference-sell into.

### Why this segment wins first

1. **Data is literally their product** — they feel data-pain more acutely than any other segment
2. **Recurring deliverables** — monthly trackers, wave reports, dashboards for clients = your product's sweet spot
3. **Your AI features hit hardest here** — Ask-Your-Data + AI narrative directly reduces analyst hours on client deliverables
4. **Tight community** — MRS (Market Research Society), AURA, Insight Platforms, Greenbook — one happy customer = three warm intros
5. **Clear economics** — Growth tier (£149/mo) = less than 2 senior analyst hours/month. Payback in week 1.
6. **AI anxiety is a tailwind** — clients are *demanding* agencies show AI-enabled work. You're the answer to that pitch.

### Firmographic filters (LinkedIn Sales Navigator / Apollo)

- **Country:** United Kingdom (England, Scotland, Wales, NI)
- **Industry:** Market Research · Research Services · Consumer Research · B2B Research
- **Headcount:** 20 — 150 employees
- **Business type:** Independent or regional agency (not global networks)
- **Exclude:** Ipsos, Kantar, GfK, Nielsen, Dynata, YouGov (enterprise-stacked) · Academic research institutions · Pure qualitative boutiques with <15 people (volume too low) · Management consultancies mis-tagged as "research"

### Buyer persona — "Anya, Insight Director"

- **Title variants:** Insight Director, Head of Analytics, Research Director, Associate Director, Senior Consultant, Head of Insight
- **Reports to:** Managing Director, Founder, or CEO (at this size, no layer in between)
- **Age / experience:** 30-45, 8-15 years in research, usually risen through analyst → senior analyst → director
- **Team:** Manages 2-8 analysts and researchers
- **A typical week:** Scoping new projects with clients, reviewing analyst output, pulling together client presentations, designing questionnaires, defending methodology, running internal training
- **Tools today:** Q / Q Research Software, SurveyMonkey or Qualtrics, SPSS, Excel, PowerPoint, sometimes a one-off Power BI dashboard built for a specific client, occasionally Tableau for a very data-forward client
- **What she owns:** End-to-end delivery of insight projects, team utilisation, client NPS, repeat-revenue from existing accounts
- **What's broken:**
  - **Client deliverables take days of Excel + PowerPoint rebuilding** every wave / quarter — same structure, no reusable pipeline
  - **Analysts spend 60% of billable time on data wrangling**, not on actually generating insight (and she knows the billable-hour leakage is eroding margin)
  - **Clients are asking for "dashboards, not decks"** and she doesn't have a tool between bespoke PowerPoint and £40k/year enterprise BI
  - **AI threat from clients** — "how are you using AI to speed up delivery?" is now a question in every new-business pitch. Having no answer loses deals.
  - **Tracking studies break when source data shape shifts** — every wave she's checking the formulas haven't silently broken
  - **Can't easily share a live view with clients between waves** — client emails: "can you just send me the updated chart?" mid-month, and it's a whole task

### Economic buyer

- At **20-50 employees**, Anya *is* the buyer. A £149/mo tool on her card, no procurement cycle.
- At **50-150 employees**, Anya influences, the **MD or Founder signs off**. The business case is simple: analyst hours saved × blended rate × 12 months. Typical agency blended rate is £55-80/hr. Saving 8 hours/month = £5,280-7,680/year vs. £1,788/year tool cost. **2.9–4.3x ROI, quantified in a single line.** Show this in the demo.
- Deal size scales with **team size**, not revenue. Growth tier lands 80% of deals.

### Trigger events (when they buy — these are when to cold-reach-out)

1. **New client win announced on LinkedIn** — especially if it's a tracker or ongoing project, not a one-off study. She's about to build the deliverable infrastructure. (LinkedIn post monitoring on agency company pages)
2. **Agency hires a "Head of Data" or "Analytics Lead"** — signal that leadership is investing in modernising delivery. New hires are buyers in first 90 days. (LinkedIn job-change alerts)
3. **Agency rebrands** or publishes thought leadership about "AI in research" or "insight beyond the deck" — they're positioning around tech-enabled delivery and need the tooling to back it up
4. **MRS Operations Awards / Research Live Awards** nominations or wins — signals an agency investing in capability
5. **Job posts for "Insight Analyst" / "Research Executive"** with "dashboard" or "Power BI" in the description — shows they're trying to solve this with hires instead of tools

### Disqualifiers (don't sell to these)

- Global networks (Ipsos, Kantar, GfK, Nielsen, Dynata, YouGov) — enterprise stack already, long sales cycles, not your beachhead
- <15 employees — often founder-led consultancies without budget or buying process
- Pure qualitative (ethnography, IDIs only) — data volume too low for a dashboard layer to matter
- Academic research labs — no commercial buying motion
- Agencies with an existing proprietary dashboard platform they sell to clients (they *are* competitors, not customers)
- US-headquartered agencies (come back in 6 months)

### Validation test for the ICP

Within 30 days of starting outbound, you should see these signals. If not, either the ICP is wrong or the message is wrong — diagnose before scaling.

- **Positive:** 25%+ reply rate to cold outreach to Insight Director / Research Director titles. Demo-to-next-step conversion >50%. Multiple prospects spontaneously mention "AI" or "dashboards for clients" as pain.
- **Negative:** <8% reply rate, or replies center on "we're already doing this in Power BI" — segment has already adopted BI tooling and we're late.

### Expansion path — after the beachhead is won (month 4+)

Once you have 5 paying insight agency customers and a tight motion, expand in this deliberate order. Do not expand before — more verticals = diluted messaging = slower closing.

1. **Strategy / management consultancies, 20-100 employees** — same data-to-deck workflow, same client-deliverable pain, same AI pressure. Natural cross-sell from the insight agency logos.
2. **PE-backed portfolio companies, £10-100m revenue** — different buyer (portco CFO / Ops Director) but same need: recurring reporting without a BI team. Sell via the PE firm to land 5-10 portcos at once.
3. **Healthcare groups — multi-site clinics, dental, physio, private GPs** — different persona (Practice Manager / Ops Director) but same "non-tech SME needs KPI dashboards" profile. Expect regulatory friction.
4. **Commercial / operations leaders at non-tech UK mid-market** (retail chains, manufacturing, distribution, £10-50m) — horizontal expansion once the core motion is tight.

Each expansion vertical gets its own 30-day validation sprint before committing to full outbound investment.

## Design partner program

### Why design partners, not customers, for the first 5 logos

You don't need revenue right now. You need **truth** — whether the product actually solves Anya's client-deliverable pain, whether the dashboards are trustworthy enough to white-label and send to paying research clients, and whether her MD will sign off on a recurring cost. Design partners give you that in exchange for a deep discount + weekly access to you.

Also: every design partner becomes a logo on your site, a quote in your sales deck, and a reference call for the next 10 prospects. In the UK insight community — where MRS members talk to each other constantly — **one enthusiastic design partner agency is worth more than £5,000 of MRR today** because of the reference-call effect.

### Program terms — the exact offer

| Term | Detail |
|---|---|
| Duration | 6 months |
| Price | Free for 3 months, then £49/month for 3 months (Starter tier, no ceiling) — converts to Growth tier from month 7 at standard pricing |
| What you give them | Weekly 30-min check-in with you (founder access). First-in-line for any feature they request. Direct WhatsApp/Slack line to you. Onboarding call (90 min). |
| What they give you | Weekly feedback (async or call). Permission to use their logo + a quote once they see value. Introduction to 1-2 peers in their network after month 2. Willingness to be a reference. |
| Exit | Either party can exit with 30 days notice. If they exit before month 6, no conversion obligation. |
| Cap | Maximum 5 design partners accepted. After 5 filled, waitlist only. Creates scarcity signal. |

### Who you want as a design partner

- **Matches Segment A ICP** — UK insight / market research agency, 20-150 people (don't take a design partner who won't convert later or whose logo won't travel)
- **Insight Director / Research Director / MD / Head of Data with buying authority or strong MD influence**
- Already visibly feeling the pain — analyst hours on deck production, client questions about AI, or live requests from clients for dashboards alongside reports
- **Credible agency logo** — no one you'd be embarrassed to put on a pitch deck. Skip agencies with reputational issues, very recent founding (<2 years, not yet established), or that look purely side-hustle
- **Not a direct competitor to a later-wave target** — e.g. don't sign a Walnut Unlimited if it means Savanta won't touch you; pick across niches (qual-heavy + quant-heavy + B2B + consumer) so the logo wall doesn't look one-note

Aim for **5 design partners across 5 different sub-specialisms** (qual, B2B, consumer, brand, tech). Diversity matters more than volume.

## Founding Growth Lead — interview scorecard

### 3-round structure

| Round | Purpose | Format | Duration | Who runs it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Phone screen | Filter for grit, commercial instinct, basic chemistry | Phone call | 30 min | YOU |
| 2. Working session | See them do the actual job | Real task, live | 60-75 min | YOU |
| 3. Founder close | Alignment on vision, comp, terms | Coffee or video | 60 min | YOU |

Reference calls after round 2, offer after round 3.

### Round 1 — Phone screen (30 min)

**Goal:** ruthless filter. 60%+ of applicants won't clear this.

**Questions (pick 5, don't ask all):**
1. *"Walk me through your last 3 roles and why you left each one."* (Looking for: progression in commercial skill, departed for growth not conflict, owns their decisions)
2. *"What's the smallest company you've ever worked at and what did you love or hate about it?"* (Filter: true early-stage appetite vs. corporate escapees)
3. *"Describe a time you had to build something from nothing — a territory, a pipeline, a new product's sales motion. What did you do week 1?"* (Looking for: bias to action, specificity)
4. *"Tell me about a deal you lost. What would you do differently?"* (Looking for: ownership, not blame)
5. *"You have a 200-account cold list. Nobody knows our product. What's your first week?"* (Looking for: structured thinking, specific sequencing, not handwaving)
6. *"How much do you think we should charge for our product?"* (Looking for: they've thought about SaaS pricing, not a clueless answer)

**Red flags:**
- Talks about marketing automation, branding, content as the primary lever (wrong for stage)
- Asks about equity before asking about the product
- Can't name 2 UK B2B SaaS companies whose GTM they admire
- Wants to hire a team before closing the first customer

**Green flags:**
- Has done cold outbound themselves recently (not just "managed" it)
- Asks to see the product
- Asks about ICP and discounts that question if you handwave
- Talks about their first 90 days unprompted

### Round 2 — Working session (75 min)

**Goal:** watch them actually do the work.

**Format:** Send them this GTM plan 24 hours before the session. In the session, give them:

- A list of 20 fictional accounts (mix of fit and non-fit)
- Candidate must: (a) score each account 1-5 for ICP fit (10 min); (b) pick their top 5 and write a first-touch email for each (25 min); (c) role-play a cold-call opener with you — you're the prospect (15 min); (d) debrief what they'd change (15 min)

**Scoring rubric (5 dimensions, 1-5 each):**

| Dimension | What 5/5 looks like |
|---|---|
| **ICP judgment** | Correctly disqualifies non-fit accounts and articulates *why* (not just "wrong industry") |
| **Message craft** | Emails are personalised, specific, and open with a non-obvious hook. No "I hope this finds you well." |
| **Discovery instinct** | In the role-play, asks questions before pitching. Uncovers pain before naming the product. |
| **Self-coaching** | In the debrief, they know what didn't land before you tell them. Shows ability to improve without hand-holding. |
| **Commercial chops** | Talks in money: pipeline, conversion, ACV, CAC payback. Not vague about numbers. |

**Pass bar:** 17+ out of 25, with no dimension below 3.

### Round 3 — Founder close (60 min)

**Goal:** mutual sell. At this point they should want the job; you should want them. This is where you lock it in.

**Cover:**
- Your honest view of the company (what's working, what's broken, what scares you)
- Their 30/60/90 day goals (specific metrics)
- Compensation (base, commission structure, equity with vesting)
- What success looks like at 6 months and 12 months
- Their honest concerns — if they don't raise any, they haven't thought hard enough

**Close the loop:**
- Agree when you'll send offer (target: within 48h of this call)
- Reference checks: 3 references, you speak to them, 15-20 min each
- Offer: written, specific, dated

### Compensation (reminder from the JD)

- Base: £35-50k (flex by experience)
- Commission: 10% of year-1 ARR closed, paid monthly on collected cash (not bookings)
- OTE at year 1: £60-80k
- Equity: 0.5-1.5% on 4-year vest, 1-year cliff
- Laptop, £100/month learning budget, all the tools they need

- *"We're running the same tracker wave every 8 weeks and it's groundhog day"* → open with **reusable templates / scheduled reports** on the Google Sheets / survey-output connector
- *"Our crosstabs live in six different SPSS outputs"* → open with the **multi-file upload + unified dataset** flow
- *"Clients are asking whether we have any AI in our process"* → lead with **Ask Your Data** and the AI-generated executive summary, then loop back to the dashboard

### Product tour — 10 min

Never walk through the product in your navigation order. Walk through it in **the order of their stated pain**. Skip 80% of the features. Show the 20% that solve what they just told you about.

**Three moments that consistently close insight agencies:**

1. **The 90-second client dashboard** — upload one of their anonymised study outputs (Q crosstab, Qualtrics CSV, SPSS export) — or if they won't share, use a segment-matched sample we've pre-loaded. Show the auto-generated dashboard in under 90 seconds. Pause. Let them react. The "oh wow, that took 90 seconds" moment is the whole demo.

2. **The "clients click this instead of opening the PPTX" moment** — share a dashboard via a public/white-labelled link. Open it on your phone mid-call. Say: *"Your client gets this link on delivery day alongside the deck. They can filter, segment, drill — and they never email your analyst asking 'what's this slide mean for the 35-44 segment?' again."* This is the retention-lift story. Insight directors feel this one hard.

3. **The "analyst time back" moment** — use the AI summariser to generate an exec summary from the uploaded data in ~10 seconds. Say: *"Your senior analyst still writes the narrative — but the first draft and the chart selection is automated. On a typical tracker wave, that's 6-10 hours back per analyst per wave."* Translate it to money if they're a director (billable hours). Translate it to capacity if they're an operator (more studies per head).

**Avoid** — do not show the budget module, the RFM analysis, the connectors page (unless they asked about data sources), or the pipelines feature. Insight directors glaze over on finance-flavoured features and it weakens positioning.

### Close — 1 min

Never leave a demo without one of these four outcomes:

1. **Trial started on real data** — *"Let's get you set up right now. Send me one anonymised study output and I'll have a working dashboard back to you in an hour. Worst case, you have a free deliverable for that client."* (This is the highest-converting close for agencies.)
2. **Next call booked with the economic buyer** — *"Based on what you've told me, I'd like to show your MD / Head of Analytics what this means for analyst capacity across the agency. Do you have 30 minutes next week with [MD name]?"*
3. **Design-partner pitch** — if they're a top-15 tier-1 account and the discovery was strong, skip the trial offer and go straight to: *"Based on this, I'd like to bring you in as one of our 5 founding agency partners — free for 6 months, you get the product plus weekly time with me to shape it, and you get preferred pricing when the design-partner window closes. Does that interest you?"*
4. **Disqualified gracefully** — *"Based on what you've shared, I don't think we're the right fit today — sounds like you've got a strong in-house data team and existing tooling that covers this. If things change in 6 months, happy to reconnect."*

**If none of those four happens, you've lost the deal** — it will not revive. Don't send "just checking in" follow-ups. Move on.

## What we are deliberately NOT doing

For the next 90 days, the following are **off the table** unless a paying customer blocks a deal on them:

- **New product features.** The only code changes allowed are: (1) bugs blocking customer use, (2) specific asks from a paying customer or design partner, (3) onboarding-flow polish if activation rate is <60%.
- **SEO / content strategy.** Too slow. Reconsider at month 6 when you have 10 customers and case studies to write about.
- **Landing page redesign.** We just shipped violet. Stop touching it.
- **Performance / infra optimisation** unless something is visibly broken. Railway is fine at this scale.
- **Hiring a second engineer.** The second hire is always a revenue person. Only consider a second engineer after MRR passes £5k/month *and* the Growth Lead is asking for dev support on a specific customer commitment.
- **Raising a seed round.** You don't need capital yet. You need 5 customers. Come back to fundraising in month 6 with a deck that says "5 paying UK insight agencies, £1-2k MRR, 90%+ activation rate, here's what we'd do with £500k."
- **Attending every event / podcast / community.** Pick one community (MRS Company Partners, AURA, or the Research Live slack). Go deep in one place, not shallow in ten. Avoid generic UK SaaS events — they're not where your buyers are.
- **Partnerships / integrations** (Xero, QuickBooks, Sage) unless a customer asks. Integration-led GTM is a trap at seed stage — it takes 6 months to build a partner motion that closes £0 revenue on its own.

## The decision tree — what to do when something breaks

Different failure modes need different responses. Don't flail.

| Signal after 4-6 weeks | Likely cause | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Low reply rate (<5%) on outbound | ICP is wrong, or personalisation is weak | Tighten ICP segment (drop the worst-performing of the 4 segments), rewrite first line, check sender reputation |
| High reply rate but no meetings booked | Subject line + opener work, close in email is weak | Move the ask earlier in the email — single CTA, specific time offered |
| Meetings book but no trials | Demo isn't landing | Rewrite demo script, cut features shown by 50%, sharpen the 3 closing moments |
| Trials but no conversions | Pricing objection or product doesn't activate | Audit activation-rate event in audit log — if <60%, onboarding is the issue, not price |
| Everything's working but MRR is slow | Pipeline is fine, deal cycle is just long at your ACV | Patience + fill more pipeline. UK insight-agency buyers typically take 4-8 weeks from first demo to close (MD sign-off + budget slot) |
